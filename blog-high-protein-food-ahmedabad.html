Why Protein Matters (And Why Most Ahmedabadis Don't Get Enough)

Protein is not just for bodybuilders. Every person needs adequate protein daily for:

  • Muscle maintenance and repair — even if you do not work out, muscle loss accelerates without protein
  • Satiety — protein keeps you full longer than carbs or fat, reducing overall calorie intake
  • Metabolism — a higher-protein diet raises your resting metabolic rate slightly
  • Hormonal health — protein is the raw material for many hormones and enzymes
  • Hair and skin health — collagen, keratin, and elastin are all proteins

The recommended daily intake is 0.8g–1.2g of protein per kg of body weight. For a 65 kg person, that is 52–78g of protein per day. Active people and gym-goers need 1.4–2g/kg, meaning 90–130g/day.

The problem? Traditional Gujarati food is heavily carbohydrate-based — rotli, rice, dal, and farsan. A typical home-cooked Gujarati thali delivers around 25–35g of protein for the whole day. That is less than half of what most people need.

Common Myths About Vegetarian Protein in Ahmedabad

Myth

"You cannot get enough protein on a vegetarian diet." — False. With the right food combinations — legumes + seeds + dairy — a vegetarian can easily hit 80–120g of protein per day. The issue is not vegetarianism; it is food selection.

Myth

"Dal is a great protein source." — Partially true. Dal is nutritious, but a typical serving of moong or toor dal has only 7–9g of protein. You would need 8–10 bowls of dal to hit a 70g protein target. Legumes like chickpeas and edamame are far more efficient.

Myth

"Protein supplements are necessary." — Not if your diet is well-designed. Whole food protein sources from a NOSH7 meal plan deliver protein with fibre, micronutrients, and antioxidants that no supplement can match.
