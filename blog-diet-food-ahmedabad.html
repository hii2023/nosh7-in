The Science of Sustainable Weight Loss

Calorie deficit - the only rule that matters

Every credible study on weight loss agrees: you lose weight when you consume fewer calories than you burn. This does not mean starving. A 300-500 calorie deficit per day leads to 0.3-0.5 kg loss per week - slow enough to be sustainable, fast enough to be motivating.

Fibre is your best friend

High-fibre foods keep you full for hours, stabilise blood sugar, and reduce total calorie intake without effort. A meal with 8-10g fibre is significantly more satiating than the same calorie meal with 2g fibre. NOSH7 bowls deliver 7-9g fibre per serving.

Protein preserves muscle

When you lose weight, you want to lose fat, not muscle. Adequate protein (18-25g per meal) ensures your body burns fat for energy rather than breaking down muscle tissue. NOSH7 delivers 18-24g protein per bowl through legumes, paneer, and seeds.
